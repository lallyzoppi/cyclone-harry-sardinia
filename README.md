🌪️ Synoptic-scale structure

Cyclone Harry likely featured a deep mid-latitude low-pressure system with a tightly packed isobaric gradient over the western Mediterranean basin.

The strong wind field observed in Sardinia is consistent with a steep horizontal pressure gradient force (PGF), which drives intense geostrophic winds around the cyclone core.

🌬️ Jet stream influence
The intensity of the system suggests upper-level support from a jet streak positioned over or near the central Mediterranean.

Jet streak dynamics likely enhanced upper-level divergence in the left exit or right entrance region, promoting cyclogenesis and sustaining the deep low-pressure core associated with Cyclone Harry.

📉 Pressure gradient force (PGF)
Surface wind intensity can be directly linked to the strength of the pressure gradient force:

Strong isobaric packing over the western Mediterranean produced enhanced geostrophic wind speeds, which were then modified by friction in the boundary layer.

The strongest winds occurred where the pressure gradient was maximized between the cyclone core and surrounding high-pressure systems.

🏔️ Orographic effects
Sardinia’s complex topography significantly modulated the wind field.

Mountainous regions such as Gennargentu (Fonni, Desulo, Seui) acted as both barriers and accelerators of airflow. Channeling effects through valleys increased local wind speeds, while ridge-top exposure enhanced gust intensity through flow separation and turbulent eddies.

🌊 Boundary layer dynamics
The observed gust enhancement is consistent with strong boundary layer turbulence associated with cyclone passage.

Convective mixing and mechanical turbulence allowed higher-momentum air from aloft to be transported downward, producing gusts significantly stronger than mean surface winds.

Final synthesis
Cyclone Harry over Sardinia represents a classic example of a Mediterranean high-impact windstorm driven by strong synoptic forcing, jet-stream support, and pronounced orographic modulation.

The event highlights the importance of multi-scale interactions in shaping extreme surface wind distributions.
